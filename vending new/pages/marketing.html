Marketing Plan

Our comprehensive strategy for Products, Promotion, and Places

Products

Vendo Pharmacy offers a comprehensive range of healthcare products and services to meet diverse customer needs

Medication Categories

GSL (General Sales List)

Common over-the-counter medications like paracetamol, vitamins, and first-aid supplies

P Medicines (Pharmacy-Only)

Medications requiring pharmacist oversight, with tele-pharmacy consultation available

Selected POM (Prescription-Only)

Chronic disease medications for repeat prescriptions with mandatory pharmacist verification

Health Products

Supplements, health monitoring devices, and wellness products

Promotion

Strategic promotional initiatives to build awareness, drive adoption, and retain customers

Social media (Instagram, TikTok)

Follow us on social media to receive news about products and services

On-machine digital promotions

Rotating screens, QR codes, instant rewards replace traditional advertising, reducing marketing costs while effectively converting walk-ins into first-time users.

Community events

Partnerships with polyclinics and schools to spread awareness

Hospital referral vouchers

Patients referred by hospitals to use the locker or vending service receive digital vouchers or credits for subsequent transactions, encouraging repeat utilisation and positioning the vending pharmacy as an integrated extension of institutional care.

Targeted follow-ups

Returning users can receive consented, targeted follow-ups such as medication reminders, seasonal health prompts (e.g. flu season alerts), or notifications about relevant health screenings and initiatives by partner institutions, strengthening continuity of care and sustained engagement

Promotional Channels

Digital Media

Social media, search ads, health forums

On-Site

Machine displays, branding, QR codes

Community

Outreach programs, partnerships

Healthcare

GP referrals, hospital networks

Places

Strategic placement in high-traffic and high-need locations for maximum accessibility and convenience

Hospitals

Deployed in main lobbies and outpatient areas of partner hospitals like NUH, serving discharged patients and visitors

High patient traffic

MRT Stations

Positioned in major transport hubs near gantries and waiting areas, capturing commuters during peak and off-peak hours

High commuter volume

Residential Estates

Installed in HDB void decks and community centers, serving elderly residents and caregivers in mature estates

Community accessibility

Shopping Malls

Located in retail zones and near pharmacies, providing convenient access for shoppers and families

Retail foot traffic

Educational Institutions

Placed at universities and polytechnics to serve students, staff, and young working adults

Student accessibility

Office Buildings

Future expansion to business districts and office complexes for working professionals

Professional convenience
